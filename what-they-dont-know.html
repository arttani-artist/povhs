What They Don't Know

By Anon

They don’t know.
They’re standing right outside my house of cards
frail as the glass heart in me,
peering through the cracks of the flimsy structure.
And they don’t know.
Their eyes bore into me
with an intensity strong rivaling the pain that adorns me,
clotting the cuts that dress my sullied skin
And they don’t know.
And I don’t know.

Their looming gazes blur with tears of my making,
blending with the burning stabs that riddle my tainted figure.
And they don’t know.
They don’t know.
They’ll never know.

That no matter how much these thorns interwine,
drawing out the sin that runs through my veins,
I’ll enjoy it.
If even for a second,
I’ll enjoy it.
And they will never know.
