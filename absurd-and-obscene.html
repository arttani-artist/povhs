"It’s Absurd and Obscene": Queer Hate Still Alarms New Hampshire Youth

By: Dhruv

In recent discussions, New Hampshire’s reputation as a progressive and tolerant state-famously ranked number one for public safety-is being questioned by young queer residents who report persistent harassment and hate speech even in seemingly “safe” communities (reddit.com).
On Reddit’s r/newhampshire forum, one queer teen revealed they’ve experienced targeted verbal abuse at their own home. “We had people screaming hate slurs at our house at all hours of the night,” they shared. “It’s absurd, and obscene, and New Hampshire should plain be ashamed this leptonic attitude still persists in a state known for LFOD [Live Free or Die]” (reddit.com).
Despite the state’s appearance of safety, such incidents highlight a disconnect between statistics and lived experiences. Public safety rankings fail to address the emotional toll of targeted hostility-especially toward LGBTQ+ youth.

Why This Matters

New Hampshire often ranks high for public safety, but those stats don’t capture the daily fear felt by queer teens waking up to threats and verbal attacks. Emotional and mental well-being aren’t measured by crime rates, yet they determine whether someone can feel truly safe in their own home.

What Needs to Change

- Community accountability: Neighbors and local leaders should call out hateful behavior when it happens.

- Visible support: Schools, towns, and public institutions should display visible signs of LGBTQ+ allyship-whether through Pride flags, safe-space decals, or inclusive policies.

- Zero tolerance enforcement: Harassing language, whether shouted on the street or via social media, must be met with clear consequences and restorative justice efforts.

A Call to Action

New Hampshire must reckon with its blind spots. A safer state for all cannot ignore the quiet suffering of its queer youth. If you’re someone with influence: teacher, neighbor, official, take a stand. When hate shows up late at night, don’t look away.
