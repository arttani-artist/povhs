U.S. Warned Israel Before Striking Iran, Signaling Coordinated Escalation

By: The Editor

New reports confirm that the United States notified Israel in advance of its coordinated airstrikes on Iranian nuclear facilities Saturday night, underscoring deepened alignment between the Trump administration and the Netanyahu government amid rising regional tensions.
According to two officials who spoke to CNN, President Donald Trump and Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu also held a direct phone call after the U.S. assault, which targeted three major Iranian nuclear sites: Fordow, Natanz, and Esfahan. The strikes, carried out by B-2 stealth bombers, mark one of the most aggressive military moves by the U.S. in the region since the 2020 killing of Iranian General Qassem Soleimani [CNN].

What Happened

The U.S. struck three nuclear sites, citing military necessity and regional stability.
B-2 bombers likely deployed from Guam, carrying GBU-57 “bunker busters” for deep-impact penetration at heavily fortified sites.
No reported casualties yet, and Iranian state media has not confirmed the full extent of the damage.

Strategic Background: Echoes of 2020

CNN’s Nick Paton Walsh notes that the military playbook echoes Trump’s 2020 decision to eliminate Soleimani-controversial at the time, but followed by limited Iranian retaliation.
"Trump has clearly remembered his experience... Iran had limited force with which to respond and its retaliation had minimal effect," writes Walsh.
Now, after a week of missile exchanges between Iran and Israel
, the U.S. may have judged that Tehran's arsenal is too depleted to mount a meaningful response. Combined with the failure of recent Geneva diplomacy efforts, Trump may have viewed this strike as a quicker, more effective route to stalling Iran’s nuclear ambitions.

What’s at Stake

Iran’s response remains unknown. Previous threats of retaliation have yet to materialize.
Diplomatic fallout expected from European allies frustrated by the abandonment of negotiations.
No clear evidence yet that the airstrikes have neutralized Iran’s bomb-making potential.
This strike also signals a major pivot away from diplomacy, with one White House official stating off record that Friday’s Geneva talks with EU nations were seen as “a waste of time” by the administration.

The Big Question

Did this operation destroy Iran’s nuclear ambitions-or simply provoke the next stage of conflict?
If the answer is anything short of definitive, analysts warn the Trump administration may have traded long-term stability for a short-term strategic win-one that could drag the U.S. deeper into confrontation across the region.
